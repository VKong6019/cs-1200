Freshman Tips!

1. Plan out your week to keep track of upcoming deadlines and exams.

2. Remember dudes and dudettes!

It is IMPORTANT to stay on top of your work. Do not, I daresay, leave work at the last minute. Especially if you have that 6 page English essay due the next morning . . .

3. Create and stick to good study habits. They'll come in handy towards midterm and final season.

4. Explore beyond the campus!

Check out the Prudential Center or Chinatown. There's a whole city to explore outside of your dorm.

5. Don't forget to eat and take care of yourself when you're studying hard for that dreadful final.

6. Last but not least, remember to have fun and enjoy your new home for the next 4 years!!!

Time Management and Prioritization

In high school, I was used to simply memorizing my daily agenda or any future assignments that I needed to do. Consequently, I would forget at least one or two assignments until the last possible minute and regret not planning out beforehand. It's not that I wasn't able to plan my tasks; I was just being lazy and never really realize its importance since I went through high school just fine. After the Time Management lesson, I realized I needed to organize my time and prioritization better. Because of the time management unit, I was able to create a month's worth of planning that I could look back to in case I was unsure I had unfinished work.

Advising 101

The Advising 101 lesson was one of the most important lessons that helped further my academic progression. The class prepared for the Spring 2019 course registration that I completely forgot about until the week before. I was pretty nervous since I did not have as much credits as some of my friends and was unsure if I could even get the classes I wanted. Fortunately, the lesson guided me through the process by creating three possible course plans I could take and trouble shoot a few errors that may occur. In the end, I wasn't entirely satisfied with my Spring schedule, but I was definitely glad the advising lesson helped me understand the registration process.

Introduction to the Field of Computer Science

Before I came to Northeastern, I had a relatively limited amount of knowledge of the Computer Science field. I came onto campus as a Combined Computer Science and Math major, but I was still unsure of what exactly I might become after college. Seeing the intoduction to Computer Science presentation, I saw how there were numerous majors that I might be interested in if I ever realized my current major wasn't fit for me. Aside from that, I realized that I developed an interest in data science field and there was a major for it, but I wanted to stay in my combined major. I talked to a CS and Math mentor at the Major Mentor event and found out that I could still be connected to data science since CS and Math are both heavy influences in the field. I was relieved and glad that I had a greater knowledge of the broad field of CS, so that I can better develop my academic and professional career.

Living in a Cyber World: The Path to a Career in the FBI

My first Passport Event was about cyber crime and how the FBI deals with different types of cyber crime. At the same time, the FBI representative elaborated on the process of becoming an FBI agent, specifically in the cyber sector. I learned that a Business Mail Compromise happens around the world, resulting in the loss of millions in dollars. For example, Xoom lost $30.8M in Jan. 2015, and its CFO was forced to resign. The idea of a career in cybercrime as an FBI agent sounded appealing, but the event helped me realize I didn't want to take a path in the cybersecurity field. Moreover, the event solidified my goal to be a part of the data science field, specifically. I acknowledge that the idea of beating cyber crime as an FBI agent sounded appealing, but I felt much more comfortable with the idea of utilizing organized data to produce meaning and information.

Intro to Github: Beginner

My class' fellow, Derek Schuster (shoutout to him), informed everyone in my class of the github events that were being held. I was interested in git and GitHub, so I went to the first event that mainly talked about the logistics of using git and how to create your own personal repository. Technically, you could think of GitHub as a social platform for a company or group of people can work on at the same time. I further looked into GitHub and found out that I could export some of my projects into GitHub using git commands in the terminal and publish them live. I was able to publish this website from Cloud9 to GitHub :). Sadly, I wasn't able to attend the intermediate event, but I look forward to utilizing GitHub more in the future!

Stress Management through Reflections and Meditiations

This event was a bit different from the previous two events that I went to in that it was more physically interactive. I learned how to deal with stress through various methods of meditiations and exercises from the event. The instructors of the event also explained the history of meditation and its ties to Hinduism. The event was only roughly 30 to 40 minutes, but I learned a few helpful tips to handle with stress. For example, I learned to maintain calmness to help me navigate through built up stress from upcoming deadlines and exams.
